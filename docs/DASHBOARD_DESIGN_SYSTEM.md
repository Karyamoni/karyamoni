Karyamoni Dashboard: Design & Experience Strategy

1. Core Philosophy: The "Anti-Template" Dashboard
   The dashboard will not look like a generic administrative panel. It will follow the Top-Design Core Principle: "Every pixel is intentional—nothing default, nothing accidental"
   . We will treat merchant data (usage metrics, return rates) as editorial content that requires a clear visual journey and high hierarchy
   .
2. Layout & Visual Framework
   Power Lines & Architecture: Establish 2–3 primary vertical "Power Lines" across a 12-column grid
   . All status indicators and metric labels must "hit" these edges to create a sense of intentionality as the merchant scrolls through their store's performance
   .
   Asymmetric Tension: Avoid uniform rows of identical cards. We will use mosaic balance (David Carson style) for the "Product Readiness Table," where different product states (Ready, Live, Missing Data) create visual energy through varied density rather than rigid symmetry
   .
   White Space as a Weapon: Use liberal padding to create "tension through restraint"
   . High-impact metrics (e.g., Return Rate Reduction) will be given massive breathing room to make them feel monumental
   .
3. Typography: Data as Architecture
   Monumental Metrics: Apply a 10:1 scale contrast
   .
   Example: A "24%" return-rate reduction should be displayed in viewport-filling, tight-tracked typography
   .
   Counterpoint: Detailed fit notes or integration diagnostics will use intimate 14px–16px body text with generous leading
   .
   Optical Alignment: Numbers in the "Try-On Usage" table must be optically aligned rather than mathematically centered to ensure the dashboard feels crafted
   .
4. Color & Atmosphere
   Monochromatic Tension: The dashboard will primarily use the Karyamoni Palette (Ink, Paper, Mist)
   .
   Functional Accents: Use the Lime and Coral accents sparingly for status alerts
   . A "Live" product will use a Lime spark, while "Missing Data" uses a Coral highlight—this follows the "Single Accent" technique to guide the eye without visual noise
   .
   Contextual Shifting: As a merchant moves from "Setup" (functional/minimal) to "Analytics" (visual/immersive), the background may shift from Paper to Mist to signal a change in content "chapters"
   .
5. Motion & Interaction (Purposeful Orchestration)
   Staggered Data Entry: When the dashboard loads, elements will not pop in. The Setup Checklist will use a choreographed, staggered reveal, followed by the Product Table
   .
   Smooth Navigation: Implement Lenis or Locomotive Scroll for a weighted, physical feel
   . Transitioning between dashboard tabs (Setup, Products, Analytics) should feel like a seamless page morph rather than a hard cut
   .
   Magnetic Micro-Interactions: Primary actions, like "Activate Karyamoni," will use magnetic button effects to pull the cursor in, signaling high craft
   .
6. Functional Sections Integration
   Based on the MVP_SPEC.md and WEBSITE_PLAN.md, the design will accommodate:
   IKAS Integration Rail: A sticky sidebar or top-rail showing the real-time status of the IKAS app permission and sync status
   .
   Product Readiness Visuals: Instead of a simple text list, use overlapping UI mockups showing how the 3D cabin will look on that specific product's page
   .
   Return-Rate Impact Placeholders: Use "huge type with simple overlaps" to present early conversion signals even before the data is fully populated, keeping the merchant inspired by the potential value
   .
7. Responsive Adaption (The Six Rules)
   The dashboard will follow the mobile simplification rules
   :
   Summarized Metrics: Massive metrics on desktop will scale down, but CTAs will remain large (60px) for ease of thumb access
   .
   6-Column Grid: Mobile dashboard layouts will use 1/3 and 2/3 offsets to avoid a "boring" center-aligned look
   .
   Stack Shifting: Vertical data relationships on desktop will shift to horizontal swipes for product cards on mobile
   .
